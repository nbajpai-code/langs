## Introduction to Hindi
Hindi (हिन्दी) is an Indo-Aryan language spoken mainly in India. It is one of the official languages of the Government of India and the lingua franca of the Hindi Belt region. It is mutually intelligible with Urdu, with which it forms the Hindustani language.

## Alphabet & Pronunciation
Hindi is written in the Devanagari script. It is highly phonetic.
- Includes distinct aspirated and unaspirated consonants (e.g., 'p' vs 'ph').
- Retroflex consonants (pronounced with the tongue curled back).
- Nasalized vowels (indicated by a dot or half-moon over the letter).

## Grammar Overview
- **Word Order:** Subject-Object-Verb (SOV).
- **Gender:** Two genders (Masculine and Feminine). All nouns possess gender.
- **Postpositions:** Hindi uses postpositions instead of prepositions (e.g., "table on" instead of "on table").
- **Verb Conjugation:** Verbs change based on gender, number, tense, and respect/formality level.
