## Introduction to Kashmiri
Kashmiri (कॉशुर / کأشُر, Koshur) is an Indo-Aryan language belonging to the Dardic subgroup. It is primarily spoken in the Kashmir Valley and Chenab Valley of Jammu and Kashmir. It has a rich poetic and literary tradition and is distinct from other major North Indian languages.

## Alphabet & Pronunciation
Kashmiri can be written in three scripts: Perso-Arabic (official and most common), Devanagari (used primarily by Kashmiri Hindus), and Sharada (the historical script, now rarely used).
- Features a complex vowel system with many central vowels not found in other Indic languages.
- The Perso-Arabic script for Kashmiri includes special diacritics to represent these unique vowel sounds.

## Grammar Overview
- **Word Order:** V2 (Verb-Second) word order, meaning the finite verb always occupies the second position in a main clause, similar to German and unlike the SOV order of Hindi/Urdu.
- **Gender:** Nouns have two genders: masculine and feminine.
- **Cases:** Nouns and pronouns inflect for several cases (Nominative, Ergative, Dative, Ablative). Kashmiri is an ergative-absolutive language in past tenses.
- **Pronominal Suffixes:** Verbs take complex suffixes that indicate the subject and sometimes the object of the sentence.
