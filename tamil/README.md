## Introduction to Tamil
Tamil (தமிழ்) is a Dravidian language natively spoken by the Tamil people of South Asia. It has official status in the Indian state of Tamil Nadu, as well as in Sri Lanka and Singapore. It is one of the longest-surviving classical languages in the world.

## Alphabet & Pronunciation
The Tamil script is an abugida consisting of 12 vowels, 18 consonants, and one special character (āytam), combining to form 216 compound characters.
- Characterized by short and long vowel distinctions.
- Contains specific retroflex sounds and distinct 'r', 'l', and 'n' sounds not found in English.

## Grammar Overview
- **Word Order:** Subject-Object-Verb (SOV).
- **Agglutinative:** Tamil adds suffixes to root words to indicate grammatical relations (case, tense, person, number).
- **Diglossia:** Significant differences exist between the formal, written language (Centamil) and the colloquial, spoken language (Kotuntamil).
