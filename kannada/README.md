## Introduction to Kannada
Kannada (ಕನ್ನಡ) is a Dravidian language spoken predominantly by the people of Karnataka in southwestern India. It is one of the classical languages of India with a rich literary history spanning over a millennium.

## Alphabet & Pronunciation
The Kannada script is an abugida consisting of 49 phonemic letters, divided into vowels (swaras), consonants (vyanjanas), and modifiers.
- Highly phonetic; words are pronounced exactly as they are written.
- Contains distinct retroflex sounds and emphasizes aspirated consonants due to Sanskrit influence.

## Grammar Overview
- **Word Order:** Subject-Object-Verb (SOV).
- **Agglutinative:** Like other Dravidian languages, grammatical functions are expressed by adding suffixes to stems.
- **Gender:** Three genders: masculine, feminine, and neuter (based on meaning rather than form).
- **Formality:** Employs different pronouns based on the level of respect and age.
