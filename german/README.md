## Introduction to German
German (Deutsch) is a West Germanic language spoken mainly in Central Europe. It is the most widely spoken and official or co-official language in Germany, Austria, Switzerland, South Tyrol in Italy, and Liechtenstein. It is known for its rich literary, scientific, and philosophical history.

## Alphabet & Pronunciation
German uses the standard 26-letter Latin alphabet, plus three umlauted vowels (Ä, Ö, Ü) and the Eszett (ß), which represents a sharp 's' sound.
- **W:** Pronounced like an English 'v'.
- **V:** Pronounced like an English 'f'.
- **Z:** Pronounced like "ts" in "cats".
- **J:** Pronounced like an English 'y'.

## Grammar Overview
- **Cases:** Nominative, Accusative, Dative, and Genitive.
- **Gendered Nouns:** Masculine (der), Feminine (die), Neuter (das). All nouns are capitalized.
- **Word Order:** Verbs typically take the second position in a main clause but move to the end in subordinate clauses.
