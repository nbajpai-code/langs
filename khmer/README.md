## Introduction to Khmer
Khmer is the official language of Cambodia, spoken by the majority of its population. It is part of the Austroasiatic language family and is notable for **not being a tonal language**, unlike its neighbors (Vietnamese, Thai, Lao).

## Alphabet & Writing System
The Khmer script is an abugida (alphasyllabary) derived from the Pallava script of India. It consists of:
- **33 Consonants**: Divided into two series ("a" series and "o" series), which dictate the pronunciation of dependent vowels.
- **24 Dependent Vowels**: Their sound changes based on the series of the consonant they attach to.
- **Independent Vowels**: Used to represent a vowel sound without a preceding consonant.
- **Diacritics & Punctuation**: Special marks that alter pronunciation or tone length.
- **Numerals**: Khmer uses its own numerals (០, ១, ២, ៣, ៤, ៥, ៦, ៧, ៨, ៩).

**Tips for writing:**
- Khmer is written from left to right.
- Words within a phrase or sentence are typically not separated by spaces; spaces are used to indicate pauses, similar to commas or periods in English.

## Grammar Overview
Khmer grammar is generally considered straightforward for English speakers:
- **Sentence Structure**: Subject-Verb-Object (SVO).
- **No Conjugation/Declension**: Verbs do not change based on tense or subject. Instead, tense is indicated by particles (e.g., *ban* for past, *nung* for future).
- **No Plurals or Gender**: Nouns do not have plural forms or gender. Quantifiers are used to indicate amount.
- **Adjectives**: Follow the noun they modify (e.g., *Srey s'aat* = Girl beautiful).

## Learning Resources

### Textbooks & Courses
- **Colloquial Cambodian (Routledge)**: An excellent, comprehensive textbook for beginners. Includes audio materials for pronunciation.
- **Cambodian for Beginners (Richard K. Gilbert)**: Good for learning basic conversational skills and starting the writing system.
- **FSI Cambodian**: A highly structured, older US government language course. It's free online and provides massive amounts of drills.

### Online Platforms & Apps
- **Ling App**: One of the best apps specifically catering to less widely taught languages like Khmer. Good for vocabulary and basic sentences.
- **Learn Khmer (by Khmer Academy)**: Online lessons and videos.
- **Italki**: Highly recommended for finding native Khmer tutors for conversational practice and personalized lessons.

### Dictionaries
- **SEALang Library Khmer**: Provides an extensive, searchable database based on the Headley dictionary.
- **Khmer Dictionary (by Chuon Nath)**: The authoritative dictionary for the Khmer language (all in Khmer script, ideal for advanced learners).
- **Bilingual Dictionary Apps**: Various apps exist on iOS and Android; look for those that include audio pronunciations.

### Media & Listening Practice
- **RFA Khmer (Radio Free Asia)**: Great for advanced learners to practice listening to formal, news-style Khmer.
- **Cambodian Pop Music & Karaoke**: Searching YouTube for "Khmer songs" or "Khmer karaoke" exposes learners to the language and culture simultaneously.
- **YouTube Channels**: There are several channels dedicated to teaching Khmer (e.g., *Dara's Khmer*, *Khmer Lesson*).
