## Introduction to Marathi
Marathi (मराठी) is an Indo-Aryan language spoken predominantly by the Marathi people of Maharashtra, India. It is the official language of Maharashtra and co-official in Goa, boasting a rich literary tradition and serving as a major language in India's financial capital, Mumbai.

## Alphabet & Pronunciation
Marathi is written in the Balbodh variant of the Devanagari script. It consists of 16 vowels and 36 consonants.
- Features some unique phonemes not found in standard Hindi, such as the retroflex lateral approximant 'ळ' (ḷ) and specific pronunciations of 'च' (ca) and 'ज' (ja).
- Vowels can be nasalized, which is marked by an anusvara (a dot).

## Grammar Overview
- **Word Order:** Subject-Object-Verb (SOV).
- **Gender:** Unique among modern Indo-Aryan languages, Marathi retains three grammatical genders from Sanskrit: masculine, feminine, and neuter.
- **Cases:** Employs a complex system of postpositions attached to the oblique form of nouns.
- **Verbs:** Highly inflected; they agree with the subject (and sometimes the object) in gender, number, and person.
