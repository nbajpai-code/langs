## Introduction to Telugu
Telugu (తెలుగు) is a Dravidian language native to India. It is predominantly spoken in the states of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. Telugu is the largest member of the Dravidian language family and one of the classical languages of India.

## Alphabet & Pronunciation
The Telugu script is an abugida consisting of 60 symbols: 16 vowels, 3 vowel modifiers, and 41 consonants.
- Telugu is often called the "Italian of the East" because words frequently end in vowels.
- Features distinct aspirated and unaspirated consonants, heavily influenced by Sanskrit.

## Grammar Overview
- **Word Order:** Subject-Object-Verb (SOV).
- **Agglutinative:** Grammatical functions are primarily expressed through suffixes attached to noun or verb roots.
- **Gender:** Masculine, feminine, and neuter.
- **Formality:** Pronouns and verbs change based on the level of respect/formality toward the listener or the person being talked about.
