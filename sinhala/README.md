## Introduction to Sinhala
Sinhala (සිංහල) is an Indo-Aryan language primarily spoken by the Sinhalese people in Sri Lanka, where it is an official and national language.

## Alphabet & Pronunciation
The Sinhala script is an abugida (an alphasyllabary). It is characterized by rounded letters.
- Features prenasalized consonants (e.g., mb, nd, ng) which are pronounced quickly.
- Vowel marks are added around the base consonants to alter the sound.
- It includes two distinct 'ae' vowel sounds not typically found in other Indo-Aryan languages.

## Grammar Overview
- **Diglossia:** Sinhala has a sharp distinction between the spoken and written forms. The written language uses complex verb conjugations and cases, while the spoken language is much simpler.
- **Word Order:** Subject-Object-Verb (SOV).
- **Verbs:** In spoken Sinhala, verbs do not inflect for person, number, or gender, only for tense.
