## Introduction to Sanskrit
Sanskrit (संस्कृतम्) is a classical language of South Asia that belongs to the Indo-Aryan branch of the Indo-European languages. It is the sacred language of Hinduism and the language of classical Hindu philosophy, as well as historical texts of Buddhism and Jainism.

## Alphabet & Pronunciation
Sanskrit is typically written in the Devanagari script. It consists of 14 vowels and 33 consonants. The pronunciation is highly phonetic and precise.
- **Vowels:** Divided into short (a, i, u, ṛ, lṛ) and long (ā, ī, ū, ṝ, e, ai, o, au).
- **Consonants:** Categorized by articulation points (guttural, palatal, cerebral, dental, labial).

## Grammar Overview
Sanskrit grammar is rigorously structured, most famously codified by the ancient grammarian Pāṇini.
- **Cases:** 8 noun cases (Nominative, Accusative, Instrumental, Dative, Ablative, Genitive, Locative, Vocative).
- **Numbers:** Singular, Dual, and Plural.
- **Verbs:** Conjugated based on tense, mood, person, and number. Roots (dhatus) form the basis of most words.
