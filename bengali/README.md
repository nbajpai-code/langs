## Introduction to Bengali
Bengali (বাংলা, Bangla) is an Indo-Aryan language primarily spoken by the Bengalis in South Asia. It is the official and most widely spoken language of Bangladesh and the second most widely spoken of the 22 scheduled languages of India (predominantly in West Bengal, Tripura, and Assam).

## Alphabet & Pronunciation
Bengali is written using the Bengali-Assamese script, an abugida consisting of 11 vowels and 39 consonants.
- The inherent vowel in consonants is typically an "o" sound (as in "hot") rather than the "a" sound found in Hindi/Sanskrit.
- Vowel sounds can change depending on their position within a word (vowel harmony).
- Does not strictly distinguish between 'v' and 'b' sounds (both are typically pronounced as 'b').

## Grammar Overview
- **Word Order:** Subject-Object-Verb (SOV).
- **Gender:** No grammatical gender for nouns or verbs.
- **Cases:** Employs postpositions and suffixes.
- **Classifiers:** Similar to Assamese, Bengali uses classifiers (e.g., 'ta' or 'ti' for objects, 'jon' for people) when quantifying nouns.
- **Verbs:** Highly inflected for tense, person, and politeness, but independent of number.
