## Introduction to Russian
Russian (русский язык) is an East Slavic language spoken natively by Russians in Eastern Europe. It is the most widely spoken Slavic language and the most widely spoken native language in Europe.

## Alphabet & Pronunciation
Russian is written using a variant of the Cyrillic alphabet consisting of 33 letters.
- **False Friends:** Some letters look like Latin but sound different (e.g., В = v, Р = r, Н = n, С = s, У = u, Х = kh).
- **Stress:** Stress is variable and mobile; an unstressed 'o' is pronounced like an 'a' (vowel reduction).
- **Hard/Soft Consonants:** Consonants can be palatalized (soft) or unpalatalized (hard), changing meaning.

## Grammar Overview
Russian grammar is complex and highly inflected.
- **Cases:** Six cases (Nominative, Genitive, Dative, Accusative, Instrumental, Prepositional).
- **Gender:** Masculine, Feminine, and Neuter.
- **Verbs:** Russian verbs have two aspects: Imperfective (ongoing/repeated action) and Perfective (completed action).
- **Word Order:** Extremely flexible, though Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) is neutral. Variations highlight new or important information.
