## Introduction to Portuguese
Portuguese (Português) is a Romance language originating in the Iberian Peninsula. It is the sole official language of Portugal, Brazil, Cape Verde, Angola, Mozambique, Guinea-Bissau, and São Tomé and Príncipe.

## Alphabet & Pronunciation
Portuguese uses the standard 26-letter Latin alphabet with several diacritics (á, â, ã, à, ç, é, ê, í, ó, ô, õ, ú).
- **Nasal Vowels:** Indicated by a tilde (ã, õ) or an 'm' or 'n' at the end of a syllable, giving Portuguese its characteristic sound.
- **Differences:** Major pronunciation differences exist between European Portuguese (more closed vowels, "sh" sounds) and Brazilian Portuguese (more open, musical intonation).

## Grammar Overview
- **Gender:** Nouns are either masculine or feminine.
- **Verbs:** Highly inflected with three main conjugations (-ar, -er, -ir). Contains a personal infinitive (an infinitive that conjugates based on the subject).
- **Pronouns:** Pronoun placement varies strictly between Brazilian and European Portuguese.
