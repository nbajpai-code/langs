## Introduction to Urdu
Urdu (اردو) is an Indo-Aryan language spoken chiefly in South Asia. It is the national language and lingua franca of Pakistan and an officially recognized language in India. It is mutually intelligible with standard Hindi, sharing a common grammar and base vocabulary, though differing in formal vocabulary and script.

## Alphabet & Pronunciation
Urdu is written right-to-left in an extension of the Persian alphabet, which is itself an extension of the Arabic alphabet. It uses the Nastaliq calligraphic style.
- Contains 39 basic letters.
- Vowels are often omitted in writing (abjad-style), requiring learners to infer pronunciation from context.
- Features distinct letters for retroflex sounds (ṭ, ḍ, ṛ) not found in Arabic or Persian.

## Grammar Overview
- **Word Order:** Subject-Object-Verb (SOV).
- **Gender:** Two genders (Masculine and Feminine).
- **Postpositions:** Like Hindi, Urdu uses postpositions instead of prepositions.
- **Politeness Levels:** Pronouns and verbs change to reflect social hierarchy, respect, and formality (Tu, Tum, Aap).
