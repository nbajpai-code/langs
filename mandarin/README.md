## Introduction to Mandarin
Mandarin (普通话, Pǔtōnghuà) is a group of Sinitic (Chinese) languages spoken across most of northern and southwestern China. Standard Mandarin is the official state language of China and Taiwan, and is one of the four official languages of Singapore.

## Pinyin & Tones
Mandarin uses characters (Hanzi) rather than an alphabet. Pinyin is the official romanization system used to learn pronunciation.
Mandarin is a tonal language, meaning the pitch of a word changes its meaning entirely. There are four main tones and one neutral tone:
- **1st Tone (Flat):** mā (mother)
- **2nd Tone (Rising):** má (hemp)
- **3rd Tone (Falling-Rising):** mǎ (horse)
- **4th Tone (Falling):** mà (scold)
- **Neutral Tone:** ma (question particle)

## Grammar Overview
- **Word Order:** Subject-Verb-Object (SVO), similar to English.
- **No Conjugation:** Verbs do not change form to indicate tense or person. Tense is indicated by time words (e.g., "yesterday", "tomorrow") or aspect particles (like 'le' 了 for completed actions).
- **Measure Words:** Required when counting objects (e.g., "yī gè rén" = one [measure word] person).
