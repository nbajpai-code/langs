## Introduction to English
English is a West Germanic language of the Indo-European language family. It is the most spoken language in the world and serves as a global lingua franca. It has immense vocabulary borrowed heavily from Latin, French, and other languages.

## Alphabet & Pronunciation
English uses the Latin alphabet consisting of 26 letters.
- Pronunciation can be notoriously irregular because words often do not sound the way they are spelled.
- Vowel sounds vary greatly across different dialects (e.g., American vs. British English).

## Grammar Overview
- **Word Order:** Subject-Verb-Object (SVO).
- **Articles:** Definite (the) and indefinite (a, an).
- **Verbs:** Relatively simple conjugation compared to Romance languages, primarily relying on auxiliary verbs (do, have, will) to indicate tense and mood.
- **Nouns:** Do not have grammatical gender. Plurals are generally formed by adding 's' or 'es'.
